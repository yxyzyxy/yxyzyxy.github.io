Exam Planetary System

for exams i use the astronomical symbols for the planets (see here)

  • - earth= perfect! home run!
  • - moon = you're very close to having perfect solution
  • - mars = you're in the right ballpark (right idea) but you made a minor error in your work or need to push your idea on a little further
  • - jupiter = you're on the right track but there's a serious error in your work
  • - saturn = you have something related going on!
  • - neptune = alright, you wrote down something related to the topic of the question
  • - pluto = a cold, desolate place that is no longer recognised as one of our own :(

    • Redo every exercise on your exam that is not marked with either an earth or a moon.

    Homework grading

    unless your syllabus says otherwise, problem sets in my classes are usually collected weekly with the following scoring guideline:

    • - each problem is worth 5 points
    • - 7 problems are randomly selected from the given problem set; these 7 are scored
    • - the top 5 scores from the 7 make up your problem set grade, graded out of a score of 25
